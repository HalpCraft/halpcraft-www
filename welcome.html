Read the full guide, not just the summary

Tell your new hire they're being nudged
Transparency builds trust. Letting your new hire know you're using a structured approach signals intentionality — that's a good thing.

Make it your own
The nudges give you the research, the timing, and the exact action to take. The wording is there as a starting point — use it as a guide and deliver it in a way that sounds like you. The most effective managers make it feel like a natural conversation, not a checklist.

Your nudges are timed around your new hire's start date. If they work non-standard days or rotating shifts, take a moment to review the calendar events after importing and shift any dates that don't land on a working day for your team. It takes two minutes and makes the timing far more relevant.
