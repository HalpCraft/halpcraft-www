The Belonging Ritual

The first hour decides more than the first month.

A new hire spends day one deciding one thing: did I make a mistake. They are reading the room for whether they belong here. Most managers fill day one with logins and paperwork. That answers a question nobody is asking. Today, your job is to make belonging unmistakable before lunch.

1
Be at the door, not in a meeting
Greet Erik yourself in the first five minutes. Presence on day one signals priority more than any welcome pack.
2
Name three people they will rely on
Introduce them by what they do and why Erik will need them. A new hire who knows who to ask stops feeling like a guest.
3
Give one real task before they leave
Something small they can finish today. Contribution, not orientation, is what makes someone feel they belong.
